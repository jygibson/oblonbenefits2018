Flexible Spending Accounts

There are three types of flexible spending accounts (FSAs) with some very clear differences.

Important Open Enrollment Reminder: Any FSA enrollments do not rollover and need to be re-elected during Open Enrollment every year. You must login to the Open Enrollment module in ADP WFN and make a contribution election for 2019 if you would like any kind of an FSA plan.

The Three Types of Flexible Spending Accounts


Standard Flexible Spending Account

Standard FSAs are pre-tax contribution accounts in which the value can be used for uncovered medical, dental, and vision costs. You can elect a value each year up to the IRS limit. Your full elected amount is available to you on the first day of the year, and then the Firm deducts a smaller amount each pay check of the year, to equal the total. For example, if you elected $2,000 for the year, $2,000 would be available for you to use on 1/1. Then, you would pay $83.30 a pay period (24 pay periods) to make up the cost.

Once enrolled, you will receive what is called a "benny" card in the mail. This card works like a debit card and holds your FSA balance. You can use your FSA with either this card, or through the Trion portal.

You need to re-enroll in the FSA every year during Open Enrollment.

If you have a Health Savings Account, you cannot have a Standard Flexible Spending Account.

Limited Purpose Flexible Spending Account

Because you cannot have a Standard Flexible Spending Account if you are enrolled in a Health Savings Account, another kind of FSA has been created: the Limited Purpose Flexible Spending Account. The LPFSA is a pre-tax contribution account that can be used in conjunction with your HSA. It works identically to the Standard FSA, however the amount can only be used for dental and vision benefits. You cannot use any part of the LPFSA for medical costs. Your full elected amount is available to you on the first day of the year, and then the firm deducts a smaller amount each pay check to equal the total.

Once enrolled, you will receive what is called a "benny" card in the mail. This card works like a debit card and holds your LPFSA balance. You can either use your LPFSA with this card, or through the Trion portal.

You need to re-enroll in your LPFSA every year during Open Enrollment.

Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account

Dependent Care FSA is rather different then the other two types. It is also a pre-tax contribution account. Dependent Care FSA is designed to help with the costs of day care for children under the age of 13 and/or family members who may be physically or mentally incapable of self care and lives in your home. There are some intricacies for how to use this plan and who you can pay with it, so be sure to check the IRS website or with your tax advisor for guidance.

Unlike the other two FSAs types, the DCFSA value is not given to you in advance. You can only use the money is it accrues, and you will not receive a "benny" card for it. You can make your payments from this account through the Trion portal.

You need to re-enroll in your DCFSA every year during open enrollment.
