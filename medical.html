Medical Insurance

Check the individual pages for plan details

Oblon offers two medical plans currently named Plan B and Plan C, both utilizing the Innovation Health/Aetna National Network. Both plans are what is considered "self-funded". Self-funded means that while the plan uses the Aetna network and Aetna acts as a third party administrator for the claims, the Firm foots the bills.

Both of the plans are high deductible plans, sometimes referred to as consumer driven. You will frequently see the acronyms HDHP or CDHP when referring to them. The biggest difference between the two plans on their face is that Plan B has higher premiums and lower deductibles/out-of-pocket maximums. Plan C has lower premiums, and higher deductibles/out-of-pocket maximums. Because both plans are HDHPs, enrolling in either makes you eligible for a Health Savings Account.

Discount prescription drugs are included with both medical plans, as well as mail-in prescription services, which also have in- and out- of-network choices.

I know what Aetna is, but what's Innovation Health?

Innovation Health is an partnership between nationally known Aetna, and locally known INOVA. This partnership means that while the entire Aetna Open POS II network is in-network for you, utilizing INOVA providers for your visits will come with even steeper discounts. When searching the Aetna site for a doctor, use "Open POS II".

What's a CDHP/HDHP?

Consumer Driven Health Plans/High Deductible Health Plans have higher deductibles than traditional plans and must meet a few government regulations. The premiums are typically much lower then standard plans, but you must reach a higher deductible before the plan cost sharing kicks in. Oblon’s CDHPs use a PPO Network, meaning your costs will be less if you stay in-network. However, you can use out-of-network providers if you prefer. When do the premiums come out of my check?

Premiums are deducted from each check, semi-monthly (which is twice a month). Our premiums are based on your earnings tier and level of coverage (employee only, employee + 1, or employee + family). You can find detailed cost information on each plan page.

When will I get my insurance card?

You will receive your card in the mail within 15 business days. If you need a card before it arrives, there are two options. You can either print out a paper card by registering with the Innovation Health portal, and downloading it from there. Or, you can use an electronic card by downloading the Aetna mobile app on your phone and signing in.
