Plan C Information

Plan C is a high deductible health plan with lower premiums, but higher deductibles and out-of-pocket maximums.

Premiums, Deductibles, Co-Insurance, and Out-of-Pocket Maximums


Please note the following:
Oblon's plans are self-funded. Self-funded plans rely on what are called "premium equivalents" and do not pay out premiums to an insurance company. In this instance, the term premium is used to more easily facilitate communications.

Deductibles and Out of Pocket values are annual amounts. These values always reset at the beginning of the year (January 1, 20xx), regardless of when in the year you began working for Oblon.

How your salary band is calculated? Salary bands are based on your W-2 earnings from the prior year. This number includes your hourly/salaried wages, any overtime worked, and/or your draw + billings. Bands are recalculated each year, and you will be notified by HR if you will be changing salary bands.

Plan C has"embedded" deductibles and out of pocket maximum: Plan C has what is called a "embedded" deductible. ‘Embedded’ plans were designed so that no single person’s costs go over their plan's individual out of pocket maximum. In 2019, the maximum out of pocket limit for individuals is $5,000. Once one of your family members reaches the individual deductible maximum ($3,000), the plan cost sharing begins for that family member (Firm pays 90%, you pay 10%). Remaining family members will need to reach the aggregate deductible with their claims before the cost sharing begins for them. This also applies to the Out-of-Pocket maximum.


Co-Insurance and Common Services


Preventive Care: Under the Affordable Care Act, preventive care is covered at 100%, as long as you meet the requirements and stay in-network.

How does it work?: Each time you go to the doctor for a service that is not preventive care, they will charge you the full Aetna-negotiated amount, until you reach your deductible. Once your deductible is met, the Plan's cost sharing begins. The Firm pays 90% and you pay 10% of claims, and prescription costs become co-pays. This continues until you reach your out-of-pocket maximum. Once you reach the out-of-pocket maximum, your services are covered at 100%.
