Individual Disability Income

Individual Disability Income (IDI) is designed to supplement your Long Term Disability.

Important Open Enrollment Notice: Individual Disability Income is not part of Open Enrollment. If you would like to cancel your coverage during Open Enrollment, please contact Human Resources.


What is individual Disability Income?

Individual Disability Income (IDI) is offered through Unum. This benefit is typically only offered to new hires, but every few years it is opened up during Open Enrollment for new applicants (not in 2019). The Firm provides employees with a Long Term Disability(LTD) plan, which you can read more about on the Firm Paid Benefits page. The LTD plan coverage provides you with 60% of your basic monthly earnings in the event of your own illness/injury that prevents you from performing your job. By enrolling in the Individual Disability Income (IDI) protection, you will be covered up to 75% of your basic monthly earnings.

How Does it Work?

The benefit payments under the IDI plan run concurrently with the Firm's LTD plan, and you will receive monthly checks if on leave.

How Can I Enroll?

New hires (or newly eligible employees) will receive personalized enrollment packets within their first few weeks. To enroll, you will need to complete the application within the given time frame (which will be designated in your packet) and return the packet to Human Resources.
