Medical Insurance

Check the individual pages for plan details

Oblon offers three medical plans named Plan B, Plan C, and Plan D, all which utilize the Innovation Health/Aetna National Network. Both plans are "self-funded". Self-funded means that while the Plans use the Aetna network, with Aetna playing the role of third party administrator fto process our claims; the Firm itself pays the bill as opposed to an insurance company.

Plans B & C are high deductible health plans, sometimes referred to as consumer driven health plans. You will frequently see the acronyms HDHP or CDHP when referring to them. The biggest difference between the two plans on their face is that Plan B has higher premiums and lower deductibles/out-of-pocket maximums. Plan C however, has lower premiums, and higher deductibles/out-of-pocket maximums. Because both plans are HDHPs, enrolling in either makes you eligible to participate in a Health Savings Account.

Plan D is new in 2020, and is a traditional PPO plan with standard co-pays. The biggest difference between the other plans and Plan D is that instead of meeting the deductible before the plan begins as with B and C, the partipant pays co-pays for most services before the deductible in D. Plan D has higher premiums and higher out-of-pocket maximums than Plans B & C, but it has co-pays and a lower deductible. Because Plan D is a traditional PPO plan, you cannot have an HSA with the plan. You can however, have a standard medical FSA.

Aetna Discounted prescription drugs are included with both medical plans, as well as mail-in prescription services, which also have in- and out- of-network choices.

Required notices about the Women's Health and Cancer Rights Act and Prescription Drugs Coverage and Medicare can be found on the Oblon intranet here. This is an Oblon internal link and will only work on the Oblon network. You may print the notices yourself, or you may request a free, hard copy of the notices by emailing Human Resources.

I know what Aetna is, but what's Innovation Health?

Innovation Health is a partnership between nationally known Aetna, and locally known INOVA. This partnership means that while the entire Aetna Open POS II network is in-network for you, utilizing INOVA providers for your visits will come with even steeper discounts. When searching the Aetna site for a doctor, use "Open POS II".

What's a CDHP/HDHP?

Consumer Driven Health Plans/High Deductible Health Plans have higher deductibles than traditional plans and must meet a few government regulations. The premiums are typically much lower than standard plans, but you must reach a higher deductible threshold before the plan cost sharing kicks in. Oblonâ€™s CDHPs use a PPO Network, meaning your costs will be less if you stay in-network. You can use out-of-network providers if you prefer, however your out-of-pocket costs will be less if you stay in-network. As mentioned above, IRS qualified CDHP/HDHPs allow a participant to also enroll in a Health Savings Account.

When do the premiums come out of my pay?

Premiums are deducted on each pay date, semi-monthly (which is twice a month). Our premiums are based on your earnings tier and level of coverage (employee only, employee + 1, or employee + family). You can find detailed cost information on each plan page.

When will I get my insurance card?

You will receive your card in the mail within 15 business days. If you need a card before it arrives, there are two options. You can either print out a paper card by registering with the Innovation Health portal, and downloading it from there, or you can use an electronic card by downloading the Aetna mobile app on your phone and signing in. We strongly recommend you register on the Innovation Health portal to take advantage of their web-tools to help find medical providers/services/prescriptions at the lowest rates. You can view claims and EOBs electronically there as well.
