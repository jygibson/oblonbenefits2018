Health Savings Accounts

Health Savings Accounts are a great way to save for future health expenses.

Health Savings Accounts: Health Savings Accounts (HSA) are savings accounts with a few unique features. First, it's a tax-preferred savings account (which means pre-tax contributions) but you can only use the funds for medical, dental, and vision purposes. All contributions are deducted on paydates, pre-tax, thus lowering your taxable income. This account's funds are yours, just like a regular savings account, and it remains in your account forever, even if you separate from the Firm. In order to be enrolled in an HSA, you must be enrolled in what is called a high deductible (or consumer driven) health plan. All of Oblon's plans meet these requirements, so if you're on one of our medical plans there is no need to worry. You can make your HSA savings work for your needs. Some people like to use it up front to pay their co-insurance bills, some people like to save it to match their deductible, and some people try not to use their HSAs at all so that the account grows large enough to use during retirement!


Another perq, you say?: An additional great feature of the HSAs is that you can invest the money sitting in your HSA, once your balance reaches $1,000. See the vendor's options for investments, and remember that any dividends your investments earn are also tax free!

How to Use Your HSA: HSAs (and FSAs) are administered through a company called Trion. You do have access to a secure online portal that holds all your account information here: Trion Portal. Once you are enrolled in an HSA, you will receive what is called a "benny" card in the mail, along with instructions for registering with the portal. The benny card acts like a debit card, and you swipe it at the point of sale. The envelope(s) will be minimally marked, so be careful not to toss it. Each benny card is good for five years. You will have an option to change your contribution amounts quarterly, which isn't mandatory, but you must re-elect the HSA each year during open enrollment to continue your contributions.

The importance of paying attention to your snail-mail: Several communications, including benny cards, come in the mail to your address from Trion. While none of us expect real mail anymore these days, it's important to pay attention to your mail for these items. The envelopes are minimally marked, with either "Trion" or "MMA Marketlink" listed on them. In addition to your cards, these communications can include instructions for registering with the portal, or if there are any issues opening your account. Under the Patriot Act, federal law requires financial institutions to obtain, verify, and record information that identifies each person who opens an account. On occasion, Trion will need more information from you to do so, and these requests will come via snail mail. Please look at your mail!

Important: Unique HSA Eligibility Features

HSAs have some unique eligiblity requirements that are different than plans you may be used to. First, in order to be eligible you must enrolled in a high deductible health plan. Secondly, you may not be enroll in any part of Medicare (Parts A, B, C or D!), you may not be enrolled in Tricare (even as secondary coverage), you may not have secondary coverage and you cannot be someone else's tax dependent.

Dependent children must be under* 24 years of age and meet the regular eligibility requirements. If the child is between the ages of 18-24, they must be a full time student.


Health Savings Accounts and Flexible Spending Accounts are administered by Trion. Account balances, payment procedures and more information can all be found in the Trion portal at: https://trion.lh1ondemand.com. Your user ID is your Lastname+Associate ID/CMS number+OBLON. For example, Smith1234OBLON.


*coverage for a handicapped child may continue past the age limits shown above - see Human Resources directly for more information.
