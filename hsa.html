Health Savings Accounts: Health Savings Accounts (HSA) are savings accounts with a few unique features. First, it's a tax-preferred savings account and you can only use the funds for medical, dental, and vision purposes. All contributions are deposited pre-tax, thus lowering your taxable income. This account is yours, just like a regular savings account, and it stays with you forever, even if you leave the Firm. In order to be enrolled in an HSA, you must be enrolled in what is called a high deductible (or consumer driven) health plan. All of Oblon's plans meet these requirements, so if you're on our medical plans there is no need to worry. You can make your HSA work for your needs. Some people like to use it up front to meet the higher deductibles and out of pockets on these types of plans. However, many people try not to use their HSAs so that they grow large enough to use during retirement!


Another perq, you say?: An additional great feature of the HSAs is that you can invest your money using one. Once your balance reaches $1,000, you can invest in the vendor's options, and any dividends your investments will accrue in your account - tax free!

How to Use Your HSA: HSAs (and FSAs) are administered through a company called Trion. You do have access to a secure online portal that holds all your account information here: Trion Portal. Once you are enrolled in an HSA, you will receive what is called a "benny" card in the mail, along with instructions for registering with the portal. The benny card acts like a debit card, and you swipe it at the point of sale. The envelope(s) will be minimally marked, so be careful not to toss it. Each benny card is good for five years. You will have an option to change your contribution amounts quarterly, which isn't mandatory, but you must re-elect the HSA each year during open enrollment to continue your contributions.

The importance of paying attention to your snail-mail: Several communications, including benny cards, come in the mail to your address from Trion. While none of us expect real mail anymore these days, it's important to pay attention to your mail for these items. The envelopes are minimally marked, with either "Trion" or "MMA Marketlink" listed on them. In addition to your cards, these communications can include instructions for registering with the portal, or if there are any issues opening your account. Under the Patriot Act, federal law requires financial institutinos to obtain, verify, and record information that identifies each person who opens an account. On occasion, Trion will need more informaiton from you to do so, and these requests will come via snail mail. Please look at your mail!

Health Savings Accounts and Flexible Spending Accounts are administered by Trion.
